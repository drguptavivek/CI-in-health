# [COUNTRY NAME]
## Final categories (what is “highest critical / protected”)
List item 1: The specific legal term (e.g., "Critical Information Infrastructure," "Operator of Essential Services").
List item 2: The primary law/regulation backing it.
##  Exact legal / regulatory criteria (what triggers inclusion)
Subsection A (The Definition): The specific text defining the scope.
Subsection B (The Thresholds): Quantitative or qualitative tests (e.g., "debilitating impact," "> 3 hours disruption").
##  Grading / tiering scheme
Description of whether the system is binary (Designated vs Non-designated) or tiered (e.g., Priority 1 vs Priority 2).
##  Incident reporting tests and thresholds
Deadlines: 24-hour / 72-hour rules.
Triggers: What counts as a reportable incident (e.g., ransom payment, patient harm).
##  Health information systems “most likely” designated
Evidence-based inference: Which specific systems (EHR, Ambulance Dispatch, etc.) fall under the rules, based on the criteria.
Distinction: Clearly label what is explicit in law vs what is expert judgement.
## Public vs Confidential
Public: Laws, regulations, general criteria.
Confidential: Specific lists of designated assets/entities (if applicable).
## Latest updates (2025–2026)
New bills, amendments, or enforcement shifts occurring in the update window.
## References ([Country Name])
Numbered list of sources with valid links.
